Objective: To prepare a batch of pancakes with optimal texture and flavor.

Materials:

  • Flour: 250.00 g (±0.01 g)
  • Sugar: 30.00 g (±0.01 g)
  • Baking powder: 10.00 g (±0.01 g)
  • Salt: 2.50 g (±0.01 g)
  • Egg: 1.00 unit (±0.01 g)
  • Milk: 200.00 mL (±0.05 mL)
  • Butter: 30.00 g (±0.01 g), melted

Procedure:

  1. In a precise 1.00 L mixing vessel, combine 250.00 g of all-purpose flour, 30.00 g of granulated sugar, 10.00 g of baking powder, and 2.50 g of sodium chloride (salt).
  2. Whisk the dry ingredients thoroughly to ensure homogeneity, using a mechanical mixer at a medium speed setting.
  3. In a separate 500.00 mL beaker, crack one (1) whole egg into the container. Using a calibrated whisk, beat the egg until a uniform consistency is achieved.
  4. Gradually add 200.00 mL of whole milk to the beaten egg, continuing to mix until completely integrated.
  5. Integrate 30.00 g of melted butter into the liquid mixture, ensuring uniform incorporation.
  6. Slowly introduce the liquid mixture into the bowl containing the dry ingredients. Stir continuously using a spatula until a smooth batter is formed, minimizing the formation of lumps.
  7. Preheat a non-stick griddle or frying pan to 180°C (±5°C). Lightly grease with a small quantity of butter or oil.
  8. Pour approximately 60.00 mL of batter onto the preheated surface per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges begin to set, approximately 2-3 minutes.
  9. Flip the pancake and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until a golden-brown color is achieved.
